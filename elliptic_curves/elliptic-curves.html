Requirements

The final grade of a Student is composed of two parts:
  • Part A will contain the number of points for Student's answers to the final test questions.
  • Part B will contain the number of points gathered by the Student during the course of the exercises.
Each part is an opportunity to gather 100 points. The final score was calculated according to the formula:

0,4*(Student's number of points from part A) + 0,6*(Student's number of points from part B).

However, the test is not compulsory this year.
Accordingly, this year two independent paths for passing the course are available, and each Student decides about the path selection:
  • Path.1: The Student decided not to take the test. Then I acknowledge that Student's knowledge of the subject is correctly reflected by the points gathered by the Student during the course of the exercises. Therefore formally, for Part A I set Student's number of points from part B, and the formula above for the final score is equivalent to:

    100%*(Student's number of points from part B)

    Then Student's global score is translated to the grade according to the assignments below.
  • Path.2: The Student takes the test. By opening the test form the Student rejects the possibility of choosing Path.1. In such a case the usual rules apply.

Finally we have the following assignments:
  • 2 for the final score in the interval [0,40)
  • 3 for the final score in the interval [40,60)
  • 3+ for the final score in the interval [60,70)
  • 4 for the final score in the interval [70,80)
  • 4+ for the final score in the interval [80,90)
  • 5 for the final score in the interval [90,100]
  • 5+ for the global score > 100

Lectures

Program of lectures

  • Field characteristic and short Weierstrass form.
  • Addition and doubling formulas.
  • Point compression and square root algorithms for prime fields.
  • Hasse theorem, what co-factor means.
  • ECDSA, ECDH.
  • Different coordinate systems: projective, jacobian, ... - cf. Explicit-Formulas Database for EC point addition and doubling
  • Fast arithmetic for a fixed base - the comb method (Lim/Lee algorithm) and a follow up paper "Improving and Extending the Lim/Lee Exponentiation Algorithm".
  • Projective coordinates leak (cf. also the paper From A to Z: Projective coordinates leakage in the wild)
  • Montgomery Ladder - timing resistance.
  • Why brainpool curves are better than NIST ones? Some fault attack and twisted curves - cf. the two papers:
    • Improved Elliptic Curve Multiplication Methods Resistant against Side Channel Attacks
    • Fault Attack on Elliptic Curve with Montgomery Ladder Implementation
  • Montgomery curves.
  • Twisted Edwards curves.
  • EdDSA
  • Pairing (if time permits :)
